Stay Safe Online – Checklist for College Students (India)

&#x20;General Safety Tips

* &#x20;Use strong, unique passwords for every account (combine letters, numbers, symbols)
* &#x20;Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on email, social media, and banking apps
* &#x20;Avoid logging into accounts on public Wi-Fi or shared computers
* &#x20;Always verify links and emails before clicking—check the sender carefully
* &#x20;Keep your devices and apps updated to avoid security vulnerabilities
* &#x20;Limit what you share online—avoid posting personal details like phone number, address, or ID cards
* &#x20;Log out from accounts after using college labs or public systems
* &#x20;Download apps only from official stores (Google Play Store / App Store)
* &#x20;Financial / UPI Safety Tips (Very Important)
* &#x20;Never share your UPI PIN or OTP with anyone—even if they claim to be from a bank
* &#x20;Remember: Receiving money does NOT require entering your UPI PIN
* &#x20;Do not scan unknown QR codes or approve random “collect requests”
* &#x20;Double-check the receiver’s name and UPI ID before sending money



&#x20;If Something Goes Wrong

* &#x20;Report immediately on the National Cyber Crime Portal: https://cybercrime.gov.in
* &#x20;Call the Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930 (for quick action in financial fraud cases)



&#x20;Quick Reminder



Stay alert, think before you click, and never rush financial decisions online.
