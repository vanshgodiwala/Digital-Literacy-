In one situation, a college student emailed their professor about an assignment deadline but wrote the message in a very informal and unclear way. The email lacked a proper subject line, greeting, and clear explanation of the issue. As a result, the professor misunderstood the request and assumed the student was being careless rather than genuinely facing a problem. This led to the student not receiving an extension and losing marks.

This issue could have been avoided with better digital communication. The student should have used a clear subject line, a polite greeting, and properly explained their situation in a structured manner. Being respectful and specific would have helped the professor understand the request correctly. This example shows how important clarity and professionalism are in digital communication.
